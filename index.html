Who Are We?

Happy Gas Improv Troupe is Kentucky’s longest running improvisational comedy troupe.



Founded in 1999 by WKU students Colin Thornton and Josh Chapman, Happy Gas has grown for nearly two decades entirely under student control. Performing both shortform and longform improv, Happy Gas operates under its motto “The root of comedy is trust.”

Where is Happy Gas headed next?



The Great Big Long Form Extravaganza


For years, Happy Gas has found comfort in the sweet, sensual arms of short form improv with shorter games such as "Clap On" or "Helping Hands." Suddenly, like Newton resting beneath the apple tree, inspiration struck us one day: what if we had an entire show dedicated to long form improv? So that's what we're doing!

Join us this Saturday, April 21, for an afternoon of long form improv, featuring three unique long forms. "Asscat," our most familiar long form, begins with a monologue and turns into a frenetic series of scenes that blend together in fun and unusual ways. "Day in the Life" allows one lucky audience member to tell us about their day before we show them how it "really happened." Lastly, we'll be debuting a brand new long form called "Happy Gas Home Movies," where we perform a full-length (edited for time) movie based off an audience suggestion. And to make it even more like the picture shows, popcorn will be provided!

Where: Gordon Wilson Hall, Lab Theatre
When: Saturday, April 21st at 5:00 P.M.
Admission: $3 per person, $5 for two
